How Scoring Works

Regular picks work like this: For each fight, you place a 100-score bet on your chosen outcome. If you're correct, the score you earn is based on the betting odds for that outcome, similar to how real sports betting works. For example, picking an underdog or an unlikely event will result in a higher score if you're right, while picking a favorite will yield a smaller return. However, if your pick is incorrect, you lose the 100-score bet you placed. This includes not only picking the wrong fighter, but also picking the wrong method of victory (e.g., decision vs. knockout). In both cases, you'll still lose the 100-score bet. This system rewards both safer and riskier picks, with higher potential scores for taking greater risks.
